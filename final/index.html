UC Berkeley | CS184 Final Project

Two Black Holes Colliding

Tony Xin, Jeremy Chow, Arjun Patrawala, Diana Qing

Abstract

In this project, we're simulating two black holes colliding. When two black holes collide, they combine to form even more massive black holes and this sends ripples through space time in the form of gravitational waves. We think this would be both a challenge and rewarding journey to attempt to recreate in a simulation.

Problem Description

The problem we are trying to solve is modeling two black holes which invovles a lot of physics simulations. One important problem is how we are going to model the light around the black hole since that's the unique thing about black holes, they have so much mass that light can't even get out. So, we would need to learn all the physics behind that and then the physics of the collision and how that sends graviational waves around and if that affects the visuals. We're going to do research into these areas and determine how these two parts interact with each other to create the simulation.

Goals and Deliverables

We want a 20 second or so video of two black holes collidading. To this end, our goals are the following:

What we plan to deliver

Implement ray tracing or path tracing algorithms to accurately simulate the bending of light near the black holes due to gravitational lensing. Apply techniques like gravitational redshift and blueshift to correctly color the light based on its wavelength shift. Render the accretion disk, a hot disk of matter that forms around black holes, using appropriate shading and texturing methods. Simulate the merger process if the black holes are close enough to collide.

What we hope to deliver

Interactive visualization tools allowing users to explore the simulated black hole collision in real-time. Integration of additional effects such as accretion disks and jets resulting from the black hole merger. Collaboration with astrophysics researchers to validate the accuracy of our simulation against theoretical models and observational data.

Schedule

Week 1: Project Planning and Research

Research relevant papers and resources on black hole collisions and computer graphics techniques. Define the scope and requirements of the simulation. Set up development environment and tools.

Week 2-3: Implementation

Develop algorithms for simulating black hole dynamics and gravitational effects. Implement rendering techniques for visualizing the collision and its aftermath. Integrate user interaction controls for exploring the simulation.

Week 4: Testing and Optimization

Conduct thorough testing of the simulation under various scenarios. Optimize performance and rendering quality. Prepare for project presentation and demonstration.
